Peleaga (M. Retezat), 2508 m

It is the highest point in the Retezat Mountains with a summit elevation of 2,509 metres above sea level. Peleaga is located in the Romanian county of Hunedoara and lies within the region of Romania known as Transylvania.

In addition to its highest peak Peleaga, the Retezat Mountains, part of the Southern Carpathians, are home to some of the highest massifs in Romania. Other important peaks in the range are Păpuşa and Retezat Peak.

The Retezat Mountains have many glacial lakes, including the largest one in Romania, Bucura Lake, which covers 8.9 ha and is situated at an elevation of 2,030 metres.
